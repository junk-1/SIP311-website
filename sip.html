SIP311 — Student Innovation Project

RouteCart

Grocery Trip Optimization — Junpyo Kim · AI & Data Science · University of Advancing Technology

The Problem

Problem Statement

Households increasingly split grocery trips across multiple nearby stores to balance price, quality, and availability, but existing tools rarely tell them whether splitting a trip is worth the added time or gas. Shoppers need a decision assistant that compares item-level options, predicts acceptable substitutions, and recommends the cheapest, fastest, or best-value route through nearby stores.

Innovation Claim

What RouteCart Does

A mobile app that takes a grocery list, identifies nearby stores, normalizes unit prices and package sizes, estimates travel cost and trip time, and returns three ranked plans.

Plan A

Cheapest

Minimizes total item cost across all stores, accounting for unit price normalization and package sizes.

Plan B

Fastest

Minimizes total trip time by optimizing routing and limiting the number of store stops.

Plan C

Best Value

Balances savings against travel time and gas cost — answers: is splitting your trip actually worth it?

Explainability & Confidence: RouteCart explains whether splitting a trip across multiple stores is worth it and shows a confidence score for price and availability data — so shoppers always understand the recommendation.

Project Description & Scope

What's Being Built

A working mobile MVP that lets a user enter a grocery list, compare stores in their vicinity, and receive ranked trip recommendations.

MVP Scope

  • 50–100 common grocery items
  • 5–10 stores in the user's vicinity
  • Comparison: 1 store vs. 3-store routes
  • Price freshness & confidence score

Budget & Resources

Small API budget allocated for maps API or preloaded route estimates. Backend built with Python and a relational database. Mobile frontend in React Native.

Target Market

Who This Is For

RouteCart creates value by reducing wasted time, lowering grocery costs, and making the "is multi-trip worth it?" decision easier to understand.

This project is intended for budget-conscious grocery shoppers who already plan their trip via manual pricing research and value savings and time.

Primary Demographics

  • Families with weekly grocery budgets
  • College students and young adults with limited disposable income
  • Coupon and deal shoppers
  • Urban/suburban shoppers with multiple stores in their vicinity

Generational Context

Prior Art & Competitive Landscape

How RouteCart Differs

Nine existing tools were evaluated. Each addresses part of the grocery problem — RouteCart uniquely combines route scoring, travel cost, and trip-split decision support.

Flipp
RouteCart: compares basket-level savings against travel time and recommends route
Aggregates weekly ads, local deals, and shopping list support.
Basket Savings
RouteCart: adds multi-store route scoring with mileage & time penalties and price confidence logic
Builds shopping lists and compares grocery prices between local stores.
Grocery Dealz
RouteCart: focuses on whether the multi-trip is worth the extra time and travel cost
Compares grocery prices and helps shoppers build carts for multiple stores.
Cart Companion
RouteCart: evaluates total-trip value across multiple stores
Organizes a grocery list into the fastest route inside a single store.
Instacart Store View
RouteCart: helps the consumer decide their route before leaving home
Helps online shoppers and fulfillment workflows deal with store availability and missing items.
Uber Eats Cart Assistant
RouteCart: applies decision support to physical nearby shopping, route value, and trip planning
Uses AI to build a grocery basket from text or images for delivery.
Grocery Routes
RouteCart: evaluates savings from cost of goods AND travel time and expense
Compares every item on a shopping list across stores and builds a single cheapest trip.
Stretch
RouteCart: evaluates whether the deals are worth extra travel time and expense
Helps consumers compare prices across local stores for the best deals.
Smart Grocery Shopping Assistant
RouteCart: is a presentable MVP for nearby physical trips with explainable route scoring
Academic concept for finding cheaper grocery options with cost optimization and substitutions.
